MyAdvisor

Project overview:

A website that curates an individual’s level of financial literacy and creates a learning plan based on their goals and level on knowledge.

  • User interface design

So what's the problem?

We discovered that there is a limited amount of educational material for financial literacy that was easy to access and understand. This likely leads to unequal opportunities to invest and save.

Our goal was to learn users’ frustrations, needs and goals in regards to financial literacy. Depending on users’ needs, we wanted to create an app/website to provide equitable learning for financial literacy. This would allow users to be more willing to invest in stocks or increase banking as they would have more knowledge.

What we found:

Here's what some of our respondents had to say...

“I think everyone's goals and capabilities are so different that an advisor would be able to help me specifically”

“[I like learning through webinars/websites because it’s] easy, accessible, and non-judgemental.”

Is there anything else out there?

I conducted secondary analysis to determine if there was a need for a platform for improving financial literacy in the way we were thinking. There were many apps on the market to assist individuals with investing but there was not much in terms of providing individuals with the tools to learn more about financial literacy in general (including topics such as budgeting, how to pay of debts in the best way, etc.).

As you can see, the website aims to curate the users’ response from the quiz to tailor topics and learning levels. Additionally, we wanted to have the option for users to speak to a financial advisor either using the chat option on the bottom right of the browser or booking a 1:1 session. This could also give banks the opportunity to pair with the website and provide their financial advisors so users could have the option to invest with a bank.

What's next...

There are always room for improvements and this website is no exception. To improve the website I would conduct further user testing and continue to iterate to make improvements. One user indicated they enjoyed the badge feature. As a result, I am currently expanding on that idea and adding more to the prototype to show a badge/rewards page. It might also be beneficial to get in contact with others who are in finance to determine what areas are missing and what we could add to enhance the website.
